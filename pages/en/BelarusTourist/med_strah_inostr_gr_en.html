Foreign citizens’ medical insurance.

Medical insurance for foreign citizens.

In order to obtain a visa and cross the Republic of Belarus State border, a foreign citizen must have an insurance policy of a foreign country that meets certain requirements or sign a medical insurance contract at checkpoints.
 If a foreign citizen does not have an insurance policy (insurance cards) that meet the specified requirements and refuse to enter into a compulsory medical insurance contract across the Republic of Belarus State border checkpoint at international airports, this foreign citizen is refused to entry the Republic of Belarus in accordance with the procedure established by legislative acts.

Foreign citizens temporarily staying or temporarily residing in the Republic of Belarus are required to have a compulsory medical insurance contract (Belgosstrakh or Beleximgarant) or a medical insurance made with a foreign insurance company for the cases of emergency medical care provided to the foreign citizens by medical institutions (hereinafter referred to as the medical insurance contract).
Criteria that must be met for the medical insurance contract:
– the name of the foreign insurance company and its location, phone numbers of the foreign insurance company or international assistance, as well as the last name, first name, and patronymic (if any) of the foreign citizen;
– extend its validity to the Republic of Belarus territory;
– be valid throughout the temporary stay or temporary residence period of a foreign citizen in the Republic of Belarus;
– provide for an insurance amount of at least 10,000 euros.

The existence of a medical insurance contract can be confirmed by an insurance policy, insurance card, or other document containing information about the medical insurance contract.
Foreign citizens who have a medical insurance contract that meets the established criteria are checked when issuing visas, and foreign citizens who are allowed free entry visa are checked at checkpoints across the State border of the Republic of Belarus.
If a foreign citizen does not have an insurance policy (insurance cards) that meet the specified requirements and refuse to enter into a compulsory medical insurance contract across the Republic of Belarus State border checkpoint at international airports, this foreign citizen is refused to entry the Republic of Belarus in accordance with the procedure established by legislative acts.
The insurance policy or insurance card presented by a foreign citizen at the border must contain the following information:
– last name, first name, patronymic (if any) of a foreign citizen, it is possible to specify in one insurance policy several insured foreign citizens who enter the Republic of Belarus together, or the insurance policy may be accompanied by a list of insured foreign citizens who are covered by the medical insurance contract;
– term of the insurance contract (the beginning and end of the insurance validity may be specified);
– the territory of action is the Republic of Belarus or the whole world.
Insurance cards without specifying the amount of insurance are accepted with the whole world validity.

Requirements for the validity of the medical insurance contract when issuing a long-term entry visa to foreign citizens:
– the medical insurance contract must be valid for the period of temporary stay or temporary residence of a foreign citizen in the Republic of Belarus;
– a foreign citizen who repeatedly visits the Republic of Belarus has the right to make a medical insurance contract for each trip, it is also possible to issue a long-term entry visa for foreign citizens if they have a medical insurance contract for the first trip.

List of people who are not subject to mandatory medical insurance:
– countries' citizens of international agreements regulating the provision of free emergency medical care (citizens of the Russian Federation);
– citizens of the Commonwealth of Independent States participants as the emergency medical care is provided free of charge in accordance with the Agreement on rendering medical assistance to citizens of States - participants of the Commonwealth of Independent States, signed in Moscow on 27 March 1997 the citizens of the Republic of Armenia, the Republic of Kazakhstan, the Kyrgyz Republic, Moldova, Tajikistan, Uzbekistan, Ukraine.

Interaction of healthcare organizations with Belarusian insurers and medical assistance when a foreign citizen applies for medical care to a medical institution or is transported to such an institution by ambulance, the medical staff must:
– establish the identity of a foreign citizen;
– find out whether the foreign citizen has an insurance policy or a document confirming the existence of a medical insurance contract that meets the criteria in case of emergency medical care;
– inform the insured person about the admission to the medical institution by the phone number specified in the insurance policy or by the known phone number of the corresponding insurer, as well as the number of his insurance policy - if the foreign citizen has a compulsory medical insurance contract;
– inform the medical assistance or insurer by phone about the insured person's admission to a medical institution, the name of the foreign insurance company with which the foreign citizen has signed a medical insurance contract, the number of the medical insurance contract (insurance policy) - if the foreign citizen has a medical insurance contract.
