Toll roads in Belarus.

The commercial operation of the BelToll toll collection system began on 1 August 2013.

The use of BelToll system will be mandatory for the drivers of:
– vehicles with a maximum laden weight no more than 3.5 tonnes; (passenger cars and passenger minibuses) registered outside the Eurasian Economic Union;
– vehicles with a maximum laden weight exceeding 3.5 tonnes; (trucks, buses and motor homes).

For the vehicles registered in the Eurasian Economic Union member states with a maximum laden weight of 3.5 tonnes and less driveway remains free.

Drivers of the following vehicles are exempt from toll payment when using the BelToll road network:
– mopeds and motorcycles;
– wheeled tractors and self-propelled vehicles registered in the Republic of Belarus;
– special operation vehicles;
– vehicles used for the purposes of ensuring the defensive capacity and law enforcement;
– fixed-route vehicles used for urban transportation of passengers;
– vehicles used for emergency response or transportation of humanitarian aid to the population.


The fee depends on the vehicle's maximum laden weight and number of axles.

The Ministry of Transport and Communications of Belarus has indicated electronic tolls fees in force for the BelToll System according to the vehicle's maximum laden weight and number of axles:
– motor vehicles with a maximum laden weight of 3.5 tonnes and less - EUR 0.040 per km;
– 2-axle motor vehicles with a maximum laden weight exceeding 3.5 tonnes - EUR 0.090 per km;
– 3-axle motor vehicles with a maximum laden weight exceeding 3.5 tonnes - EUR 0.115 per km;
– 4-axle (and more) motor vehicles with a maximum laden weight exceeding 3.5 tonnes - EUR 0.145 per km.

For the vehicles registered in the Eurasian Economic Union member states with a maximum laden weight of 3.5 tonnes and less driveway remains free.

To use the network of toll roads covered by the BelToll system it is mandatory to equip the vehicle with a small electronic device, called on-board unit (OBU).

In order to pay for the driveaway, drivers need to register in the BelToll system by signing a contract and taking an electronic payment device on deposit. This can be done at one of the customer service points (addresses and a work schedule are also available on the website www.beltoll.by).

You can quickly get information about the BelToll operation upon the vehicles that are subject to payment obligations, current fares and the installation of an on-Board device by calling +375 (172) 798 798, which is open 24/7.
